Effects of habitual coffee consumption

Although caffeine is a common and accepted drug, it is a mind-altering substance and can cause undesired side effects, especially for caffeine intolerant people. In this essay I'm calculating how habitual coffee consumption changes the baseline level of caffeine in my body.

Motivation

I experience the usual and well-documented caffeine side effects when I consume "too much" coffee:

  • anxiety
  • irritability
  • jittery feeling
  • decreased focus

My quest here is to have a better understanding of caffeine and its effects on me.

Coffee metabolism

CYP1A2 is a gene responsible of caffeine's metabolism. People will metabolise caffeine faster or slower, depending on their genotype. Caffeine's half-life in human body is around 5 hours, but it varies between 1.5 and 9.5 hours

My CYP1A2 gene's genotype is CA, which makes me slow caffeine metaboliser. I couldn't find exact half-life data for my allele, but this paper states that it can be up to 9.5h. I'm going to assume that my body processes half of the caffeine in 9.5h.

> caffeine's elimination half-life may range between 1.5 and 9.5 hours.

Consuming the same amount of coffee every day

The amount of caffeine from one cup is exponentially decreasing, but some of it will be present in the blood. How does constant coffee consumption change this? What if I drink a cup every day?

At the point I finish my daily cup, I have

  • that cup in my body (1)
  • plus the 17.36% of caffeine from the previous day
  • plus 3.01% from two days ago
  • ...

In the long run, 0.21 cup of coffee will be in my blood additionally to the daily cup. This leads to not just a higher level of caffeine compared to when I drink coffee sporadically, but also means my body is unable to deplete the caffeine below the 0.21 cup level. This 0.21 cup will be our new baseline.

Introducing one recovery day

If I keep absent from coffee for a day I am giving my body 2 days time to metabolise coffee.

This gives 0.031 cup of caffeine as my new baseline which is almost 1/7th of the original 0.21 cup.

Conclusion

If you have caffeine intolerance or just want to avoid building coffee tolerance, you might benefit caffeine free days.
